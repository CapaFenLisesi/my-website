About me

I am a soon-to-be-finished PhD candidate at the 3D geoinformation group of the Delft University of Technology in the Netherlands, now working remotely from Mexico City. I did most of my PhD and the MSc in Geomatics programme in Delft, and the BSc in Computer Science and Technology from the Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education in Mexico City.

Much of my current research is about higher-dimensional (4D and higher) data models, data structures and algorithms for Geographic Information Systems. I am also interested in other topics that combine geometric/topological computing and real-world spatial information, such as the validation and repair of geographic data, point clouds and surface reconstruction, topological data structures, and visualising spatial data. I like theoretical work, but I strongly believe in implementing my ideas in actual working (free) software.

Apart from my work, I like many things: (certain) music, travel, photography, progressive politics, human rights and privacy, literature, science and technology, languages, trains and public transport, geography, typography and urbanism (in no particular order). I am always willing and happy to have a good coffee and a nice chat about work or any of these topics.
