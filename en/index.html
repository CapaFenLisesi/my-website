About me

I am a PhD candidate at the 3D geoinformation group of the Delft University of Technology, in the Netherlands. I graduated from the MSc in Geomatics programme in Delft, and the BSc in Computer Science and Technology from the Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education in Mexico City, where I was born and lived until 2008.

Most of my current research is about higher dimensional (4D and higher) data models, data structures and algorithms for GIS. I am also interested in other topics that combine geometric/topological computing and GIS, such as the validation and repair of data, working with point clouds and surface reconstruction, topological data structures, and visualising spatial data. I like theoretical work, but I strongly believe in implementing my ideas in actual working (free) software.

Apart from my work, I love many things: music, travel, photography, politics, human rights and privacy, literature, science and technology, languages, trains and public transport, geography, math, urbanism and cinema (in no particular order). I am always willing and happy to have a good coffee and nice chat about work or any of these topics.
